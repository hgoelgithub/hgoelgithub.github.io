Professional summary

Experienced Computational Scientist with over 7 years of interdisciplinary experience in computational chemistry, biology, and toxicology. Possesses a strong background in computer-aided drug design, predictive toxicology, cheminformatics, bioinformatics, toxicogenomics, molecular dynamics, Monte Carlo, DFT, RNA-Seq, high-throughput screening, electrophysiological, in vivo and in vitro assays, multi-OMICS, NAM, and real-world datasets. Skilled in integrating diverse experimental data to inform translational research and drug design workflows using advanced AI/ML techniques and statistical analysis. Proficient in deep learning, transfer learning, generative AI, LLM, RAG, and agentic AI applied to real-world applications. Demonstrates a strong ability to collaborate with cross-functional teams, including biologists, chemists, toxicologists, and software engineers, to validate in silico findings and develop robust computational pipelines that enhance data interpretation and translational impact.

Work experience

Computational Research Scientist II

BHSAI, Henry M. Jackson Foundation for the Advancement of Military Medicine · Frederick, MD Feb 2022 – Mar 2025

  • Data curation & visualization: Managed and visualized large-scale biological and chemical datasets, performing feature importance, data cleaning, PCA, t-SNE, UMAP, clustering, heatmaps, pattern recognition, and statistical interpretation
  • Generative AI in drug discovery: Utilized generative AI techniques to generate novel molecular structures, enhancing model predictions and supporting drug repurposing applications
  • ML models for compound classification: Developed ML models (traditional ML, DNNs, GNNs, transformer-based LLMs) to classify active/inactive compounds for neurotoxicity-relevant GPCR targets using public databases
  • Computational neurotoxicity assessment: Neurotoxicity assessment of 200+ chemicals based on high-dimensional Multi-Electrode Array (MEA) assay data (electrical activity of neurons)
  • Integrative computational neurotoxicity profiling: Integrated high-throughput data with dose-response modeling (tcplfit2) to characterize neurotoxic effects; explored chemical structure–MEA response profile relationships; incorporated chemical–protein interactions and molecular features
  • Toxicogenomics studies: Developed and implemented RNA-Seq pipelines for in vivo and in vitro animal (rat, guinea pig) and human (pooled hepatocyte) data; conducted DEG analysis for liver/kidney injury biomarkers; performed in vitro–in vivo correlation and cross-species analysis
  • Integrating genomics, bioinformatics, and toxicology to enhance understanding of chemical impact on human health and improve safety assessments
  • ADMET profiling with vNN-ADMET/SwissADME for risk assessment of chemical compounds
  • Supported web development and program upgrades in collaboration with the IT team
  • Collaborated with cross-functional teams including biologists, toxicologists, and software engineers on DoD, DTRA, NIH, and AFRL funded projects

Postdoctoral Researcher

Computer-Aided Drug Design Center, University of Maryland Baltimore · Baltimore, MD May 2018 – Feb 2022 Advisor: Prof. Alexander D. MacKerell, Jr.

  • Computational drug discovery framework: Spearheaded development and deployment of workflows supporting early-phase drug discovery — protein structure preparation, SILCS FragMaps, hotspot analysis, pharmacophore modeling, virtual screening, molecular docking, and SAR analysis
  • Protein-ligand binding affinity: Employed SILCS technology to compute protein-ligand relative binding affinities and benchmarked against FEP methods
  • Polarizable force field and MD simulations: Designed SILCS workflow for polarizable force fields and MD to assess protein-ligand energetics, molecular interactions, and docking scores
  • Water network modeling: Developed and validated SILCS-MC Water — a novel technique to accurately predict water networks in binding pockets and their impact on ligand binding affinity and pose
  • Imaging probe development: Investigated alpha-synuclein fibril binding using polarizable SILCS calculations and SAR analysis to elucidate probe–target interactions
  • COVID-19 drug discovery: Applied SILCS-based CADD workflow targeting SARS-CoV-2 spike protein for antiviral drug development
  • Target-specific modeling: Designed predictive models for hERG1 blockade integrating SILCS modeling and physicochemical properties to support safety pharmacology profiling
  • Partnered with multidisciplinary teams to facilitate data-driven decision making and translate in silico predictions to actionable insights

Research Assistant

Mississippi State University · Starkville, MS Aug 2013 – May 2018 Advisor: Prof. Neeraj Rai Thesis: Modeling Phase and Sorption Equilibria using First Principles Simulations

  • VLE & thermodynamic properties: Predicted vapor–liquid equilibria (VLE), saturated vapor pressures, critical properties, boiling points, and structural properties via DFT/classical Monte Carlo simulations
  • Adsorption & transport in ionic liquids: Predicted adsorption equilibria, transport properties, vibrational spectra, and structural mechanisms for ionic liquids with gases via first-principles simulations
  • DFT exchange-correlation functionals: Employed multiple DFT functionals for computing condensed-phase properties and dimer energetics with high accuracy
  • Algorithm development in CP2K: Developed novel “cluster translation moves” algorithm for sampling strongly associating fluids in the CP2K MC suite
  • Force field development: Classical TraPPE force field development for organic molecules
  • Partition coefficient & free energy prediction: Predicted octanol-water partition coefficients and hydration/solvation free energies for NDMA and trichloroethylene
